## P0 BLOCKERS

**WRONG** (factual / structural errors — fix first):

1. **image1.png is an AI-detector tool screenshot in the article body** — Conclusion section — Delete the image entirely before publishing, or replace with a real diagram (e.g., the 5-part structure as an infographic). (Comment 1)
2. **DocSend stat is from 2015 (11 years stale).** The cited "200 pitch decks / $360M / 3 min 44 sec" comes from a June 2015 TechCrunch article. DocSend has published refreshed pitch-deck reports (2022 onward). Either replace with the latest figure or note "in DocSend's original 2015 study." (Comment 15)
3. **"SBA recommends … about 10% of your full plan" is misattributed.** The SBA only recommends 1–2 pages; the 10% rule is general business-plan advice (Tim Berry/LivePlan), not from the SBA. Either drop "or about 10%" or reattribute. (Comment 9)
4. **"Y Combinator tells founders bluntly that investors are pattern-matchers" is a fabricated citation.** Pattern-matching is a Paul Graham idea (essay: "Black Swan Farming"), not documented YC guidance. Reattribute or drop. (Comment 16)
5. **Key Takeaways list parses as 6 items, not 5.** The Oxford-comma punctuation in "business model, and traction, and team and funding" reads as "business model" + "traction" + "team and funding." Fix to use semicolons or rephrase. (Comment 4) — same issue repeats inside the ChatGPT prompt example. (Comment 35)
6. **Closing CTA promises a downloadable template the article doesn't deliver.** "Start with the executive summary template … you can edit in Word or Google Docs" — there's no template. Either link to the real upmetrics.co/business-plan-template asset or rephrase. (Comments 27, 28)
7. **Intro/closer contradiction.** Intro assumes the reader has finished the plan; closer tells them to "finish the plan you started." Pick one. (Comment 2)
8. **Duplicate example sentences.** The "Acme Robotics opener" and the "We charge $4,000…" sentence both appear verbatim in two different sections, presented as different illustrations. (Comments 5, 20, 21)
9. **"Tips for writing an effective executive summary" is plural but contains only one tip.** Either rename singular or add 2–3 more tips. (Comment 24)
10. **"Introduction" H2 doesn't follow Upmetrics convention.** The lede should sit directly under the H1, no heading. (Comment 3)
11. **Punctuation error: hyphen-with-spaces used as a separator** (`12-18 months to implement - that's beyond the budget`). Should be a comma. (Comment 12)
12. **Missing comma before "but":** "Yes, ChatGPT can draft an executive summary but you shouldn't…" — needs a comma before "but" to join two independent clauses. (Comment 33)
13. **Banned word "paradigm."** "Disrupting the legacy paradigm" — replace with a different jargon example. (Comment 25)

**WEAK** (content present but too thin or risky as-is):

1. **"Mid-market 3PLs are caught between two bad options" passive lead-in is AI-padding** — recommend cutting and starting with the substance. (Comment 13)
2. **Team-size inconsistency in the Acme example.** Business Overview says "14-person team"; the math from Funding Use + named hires totals ~11 named, with VP of Sales not yet hired. Reconcile current vs. post-funding size. (Comment 38)
3. **"$1.3M ARR / 12 customers" Series-A bar is unsourced.** Defensible but on the low end for B2B SaaS — adding an external benchmark source would harden it. (Comment 26)
4. **"📖 Also Read:" callout pattern.** Confirm WordPress renders this as the styled callout box, not just italic text. (Comment 23)
