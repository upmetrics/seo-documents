## P0 BLOCKERS

**WRONG** (factual/structural errors — fix first):
1. Southwest Airlines vision quote uses outdated "most flown" phrasing — Vision statement examples — Update to "most loved, most efficient, and most profitable" (current official wording).
2. Image2.png (5 elements infographic) labels don't match the H3 labels below it — What makes a good vision statement — Either rebuild image or rename H3s; they currently disagree on what the five elements are.
3. Image4.png hero illustration headline says "for Business Plan" (missing article) — End of article — Fix grammar AND consider moving it to the top of the article, not after the FAQ.
4. "Does it sound like it's 'bigger than we can do' but 'bigger than we need?'" is logically contradictory — Review and revise step — Rewrite so the two halves contrast (stretch vs. believable).
5. "Make it a present one, not an eternal one" contradicts the article's own future-focus thesis — When to revisit your vision statement — Rewrite to "treat it as a living statement, not a permanent one."
6. Heilmeier Catechism sentence is grammatically awkward AND frames the catechism as a research-funding tool — How to write step 3 — Rewrite per inline comment.
7. The "When to revisit your vision statement?" H3 makes the article carry 8 sub-steps under the H2 "How to write…" while the meta title promises **7 Steps** — Lift to H2.

**MISSING** (critical absent content — fill second):
1. **AI prompt is literally not in the document.** The section "Use this AI prompt to improve your vision statement" promises the prompt and delivers a blank line where it should be. Either paste a real, copy-ready prompt or remove the section.
2. **5 small-business vision statement examples** (the brief's #1 declared differentiator — "every competitor uses only Fortune-500 examples; we won't") are absent. Add a new H2 or H3 group of 5 SMB visions.
3. **5 of 6 required internal links missing.** Only the Upmetrics homepage link in the conclusion is present. The 6 required by the brief are listed in the Links section below.
4. **Template download CTA box** above the "How to write…" H2 is missing — captures "vision statement template" supporting keyword (231 GSC impressions).
5. **"elements of a vision statement"** must-include keyword (249 impressions, position 9 — striking distance) does not appear anywhere in the article.
6. **FAQ does not include "What is the difference between a vision statement and a mission statement?"** — listed in the brief's Questions to Answer and would double the snippet surface on a high-volume question.

**WEAK** (too shallow — deepen third):
1. Several "vision" examples (Nike, Shopify, TED) are actually **missions** per their company's own sites — the article teaches readers to separate vision from mission but mixes them when it counts. Section title or framing needs to reconcile.
2. Conclusion (107 words) was flagged in the brief as "generic" — the rewrite is still essentially generic; needs a checklist, a concrete next step, and a content-led CTA.
3. Heilmeier Catechism is described as 6 questions; actual is 8. The two skipped questions ("What are the risks?" / "How much will it cost?") are the most useful translations for a business-plan reader.
4. Vision-vs-Mission table (image3.png) introduces 5-to-10-year timeframe, mission-statement defining question, and audience split — three pieces of data the body prose never mentions.
