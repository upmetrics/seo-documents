## 2. Why We'll Win

> The existing page ranks for plural variants ("business plan products and services example" pos 4.2, "business products and services" pos 10.9) with near-zero CTR. The page surfaces in search but readers bounce. The rewrite fixes the angle — not the SEO.

- **Reframe vs. checklist.** Every page in the SERP teaches "what to include." Ours teaches what each element must *prove*. Three questions — Will customers buy this? Can we deliver it consistently? Does it support the financial model? — every section maps to one. This is the gap.
- **Consultant tone, not template tone.** The article reads like a consultant reviewing a draft, not a writer summarizing a chapter. Decisive language ("This sentence is fine. This one will get you rejected."), real critique, no filler.
- **Decision-maker lens.** SERP competitors write for the founder. We write for the founder *while showing them how a lender, investor, partner, or internal exec actually reads this section*. That's the proof framing.
- **Examples by business type that show contrast.** Not "here's a SaaS example, here's a restaurant example" listed in parallel. Show how the same three questions look completely different for each — the operational proof for a restaurant is supplier contracts; for a SaaS it's uptime and infrastructure. The contrast teaches the framework.
- **A template that's actually a checklist for the three questions** — not a paragraph-filler template. The deliverable matches the framework.

## Appendix: Banned AI Terms

Writer must NOT use: unlock, unleash, harness, leverage (verb), elevate, delve, dive into, deep dive, navigate (metaphorical), landscape, realm, tapestry, beacon, game-changer, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, next-level, seamless(ly), robust, holistic, empower, supercharge, revolutionize, transform (hyperbolic), journey (metaphorical), bustling, thriving, vibrant, comprehensive guide, ultimate guide, definitive guide (in body copy), moreover, furthermore, additionally, in essence, foster, facilitate, streamline (filler), myriad, plethora, multifaceted, pivotal, paramount, indispensable, crucial/essential (empty emphasis), firstly/secondly/lastly, ensuring, utilizing, noteworthy, remarkably, undoubtedly, rest assured, with that said, key takeaways, actionable insights, in today's [X] world, it's important to note, it's worth mentioning, when it comes to, in the world of, look no further, whether you're a...or a..., not just...but also, it goes without saying, take [X] to the next level, tailored to your needs, in conclusion, to sum up.

**Use instead:** plain verbs, specific nouns, real numbers, direct statements, conversational phrasing. Write like a consultant talking to a smart founder over coffee — not like an SEO article.
