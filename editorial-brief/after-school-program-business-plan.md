## 2. Thesis & Key Messages

### Thesis Statement

> BrightPath Learning Center presents a realistic, loan-ready business plan showing how a husband-and-wife team with deep experience in education and childcare operations can profitably serve the underserved after-school market in a growing suburban Oregon community.

### Key Messages

1. Cloverdale has a measurable gap in structured after-school options — existing programs are either waitlisted, recreation-only, or school-run with limited hours
2. A combined academic-support + enrichment model (STEM, arts, leadership) commands premium tuition because parents are paying for outcomes, not just supervision
3. With $175,000 in startup capital and a realistic enrollment ramp, BrightPath reaches profitability in Year 2 and generates $48,000+ net income by Year 3
4. Regulatory compliance (staff-to-child ratios, facility licensing) is a barrier to entry that protects established operators from casual competition
5. Year-round revenue (school-year tuition + summer/break camps) smooths seasonal cash flow better than school-year-only programs

- Use education and childcare industry terminology: "enrollment," "tuition," "staff-to-child ratio," "enrichment programming," "curriculum modules," "licensed capacity"

- **Target reading level:** Grade 10-11 (business audience)
- Short paragraphs (3-5 sentences max)
