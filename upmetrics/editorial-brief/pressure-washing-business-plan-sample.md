## 2. Thesis & Key Messages

### Thesis Statement

> BrightBlast Pressure Washing LLC seeks $135,000 in SBA 7(a) financing to launch a residential and light-commercial pressure washing operation in Brookhaven, North Carolina, projecting $215,000 in Year 1 revenue, profitability by Year 2, and a 26% net margin by Year 3.

### Key Messages

1. The Raleigh-Durham metro is under-served by trusted local pressure washing operators — franchises chase enterprise contracts and one-truck operators lack the capacity for HOA work, leaving a gap BrightBlast is built to fill.
2. The financial model is conservative and lender-grade: 30%+ year-over-year revenue growth from volume (not pricing assumptions), 68% gross margin in line with PWNA benchmarks, and 26% net margin by Year 3.
3. Marcus Bennett's 14 years of cleaning operations experience and PWNA certification de-risk the management capability questions lenders raise on first-time owner-operator plans.
4. The plan accounts for environmental compliance from day one — a $4,500 EPA-compliant wastewater reclaim system is in the startup costs, not deferred — eliminating the regulatory risk that ends most pressure washing startups in their second year.
5. By Year 3, BrightBlast will support three full-time crew members beyond the owner, generate $377,000 in annual revenue, and end the year with $252,000 in cash — positioning for a second crew or a recurring-revenue commercial division.
