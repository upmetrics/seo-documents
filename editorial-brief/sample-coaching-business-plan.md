## 2. Thesis & Key Messages

### Thesis Statement

> A coaching business built on diversified revenue streams — individual sessions, group programs, corporate contracts, and digital products — can achieve profitability within 18 months on a modest $90,000 startup budget, provided the founder brings certified expertise and targets a defined local market with unmet demand.

### Key Messages

1. The coaching industry has grown 54% since 2019, creating real demand — but also real competition that requires a structured business plan to stand out
2. A blended revenue model (1-on-1 + group + corporate + online) protects against client concentration risk and creates multiple growth levers
3. Coaching businesses have naturally high gross margins (~86%) due to low direct costs, but the real challenge is covering fixed overhead in Year 1
4. An SBA loan is a viable funding path for coaching practices when backed by specific financial projections and a clear break-even timeline
5. ICF certification and niche market positioning (mid-career professionals, not "everyone") are the two strongest competitive differentiators

## 8. Content Outline

### Writing Tone

- Write like a professional business consultant drafting a formal plan for a client
- Third-person throughout — "Catalyst Coaching Group will..." not "We will..." or "I will..."
- Every claim must be backed by a specific number, date, or named detail
- Financial sections must include the actual pre-calculated tables from Section 6B — do NOT invent new numbers
- Replace vague language with specifics: "20% year-over-year revenue growth" not "significant growth"
- Use coaching industry terminology: session packages, group cohorts, ICF certification, client retention, booking flow, assessment tools
- The plan should read as if it could be submitted to Ridgemont Community Bank for an SBA 7(a) loan
- Maintain consistent use of "Catalyst Coaching Group," "Maya Thornton," and "Ridgemont, Oregon" throughout

**General rules:**
- **Target reading level:** Grade 8-9 (Flesch-Kincaid)
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
- Include real examples, numbers, and specifics
- NO filler sentences, NO passive voice, NO AI buzzwords
- See BANNED AI TERMS list in the Appendix

### Section Priority Labels

- 🔴 **MUST HAVE** — Non-negotiable. Essential to the plan's credibility with lenders.
- 🟡 **SHOULD HAVE** — Important for depth. Cutting weakens the plan but doesn't invalidate it.
- 🟢 **NICE TO HAVE** — Adds differentiation. Writer can trim if word count runs over.
