## 2. Thesis & Key Messages

### Thesis Statement

> Meridian Language Academy LLC is a Hartfield, Oregon-based private language school seeking $90,000 in SBA 7(a) financing to launch a two-classroom instruction center offering Spanish, Mandarin, French, and ESL courses — with projected Year 2 profitability driven by a diversified revenue model across group enrollment, private tutoring, and corporate training contracts.

### Key Messages

1. The US private language instruction market is growing — demand from immigrants, international professionals, and corporate clients is outpacing what community colleges and app-based learning tools can serve.
2. Meridian's three-stream revenue model (group courses + tutoring + corporate) insulates it from enrollment fluctuations that make single-stream language schools fragile.
3. With $125,000 in startup capital, 2 owner-instructors with 23 combined years of domain experience, and a clear break-even path by Year 2, Meridian presents a bankable business case.
4. The financial projections are conservative — Year 1 revenue of $148,000 assumes 55% classroom occupancy, not 100%, and grows at 30% in Year 2 as corporate contracts scale.
5. Elena and Marcus structure the school to reduce owner-dependency through documented curriculum, standardized teaching methods, and a hiring pipeline — making it scalable beyond its founders.

## 8. Content Outline

### Writing Tone

**For Sample Business Plan:**
- Write in third person throughout — "Meridian Language Academy will..." not "we will..."
- Every claim backed by a specific number, date, or named individual — no vague language
- Professional and formal — reads like a document prepared by a business plan consultant for a bank, not a blog post or FAQ
- Industry-appropriate vocabulary: "enrollment cycle," "student retention rate," "occupancy rate," "contract-month," "direct instruction hours," "per-session fee"
- Financial sections: use tables as provided in Section 6B — do NOT invent new numbers or modify the math
- Use Elena Vasquez and Marcus Chen by name throughout — do not refer to them as "the owners" without first establishing their names in each chapter

**General rules:**
- Target reading level: Grade 9 (Flesch-Kincaid)
- Short, declarative sentences in narrative sections
- No filler language, no AI buzzwords (see Appendix)
- Tables preferred over bullet lists for comparative data

### Section Priority Labels

- 🔴 **MUST HAVE** — Non-negotiable. Essential to the SBA loan narrative.
- 🟡 **SHOULD HAVE** — Strengthens the plan; cutting weakens its credibility.
- 🟢 **NICE TO HAVE** — Adds depth but can be condensed if word count runs over.
