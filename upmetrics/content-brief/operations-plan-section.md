## Appendix A — Writing Tone Guide (Specific to This Article)

**Voice:** Direct, practical, expert. The reader is stuck mid-task — they want to GET this section written, not learn about operational planning as a philosophy.

**DO:**
- Use real numbers in examples ("$42,000 monthly labor cost", "180 covers/day", not "high labor cost" or "reasonable volume")
- Address the reader directly ("Your operations plan should...")
- Frame everything in the context of "this is a section of your business plan that goes to lenders/investors"
- Use industry-typical metrics with citations

**DON'T:**
- Use banned AI terms (see Appendix B)
- Use stock SaaS-blog hedging ("operational planning is a critical practice that enables...")
- Treat this as generic operational planning — it's specifically the BUSINESS PLAN section
- Use "FreshPaws"-style fictional companies with vague numbers — be specific enough to be useful
