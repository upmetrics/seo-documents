## WHAT'S WORKING

- **Lifecycle ordering of the 9 strategies** (first sale → repeat → advocate) is the brief's promised differentiator vs. LivePlan's flat list — and it lands. The explicit "run them in the wrong order and you waste money asking for referrals from people who have not yet had a second good experience" is exactly the practitioner insight a small-business reader needs.
- **Sample loyalty budget table** ($3,000 monthly, 70/30 split, named line items) is the strongest single section. It delivers the "budget for loyalty inside your marketing plan" angle that the gap table flagged as a unique edge, and it gives lenders something concrete to evaluate.
- **Real-dollar math throughout** ($30 ad CAC vs. $5 retention, 10% discount on $50 = 1/3 of $15 gross profit, $0.50 thank-you cards × 1,000 = $500) keeps the article from drifting into the abstract enterprise advice the brief warned against.
- **Owner-operator FAQ design** — six questions answered concretely (budget %, software optional, time horizon, discounts vs. rewards, customer vs. brand loyalty) matches the brief's "questions a small business owner actually types post-read" promise.
- **Small-business framing held throughout** — no assumption of CRM/CDP infrastructure, references Tidio/Help Scout/Square/Google Forms as lo-fi tooling, mentions punch cards as a legitimate option.

## P0 BLOCKERS

**WRONG** (factual / contradiction errors — fix first):
1. **8 C's of customer loyalty list is fabricated** — FAQ "What are the 8 c's of customer loyalty?" — the article lists Confidence, Commitment, Contentment, Closeness, Communication, Customization, Convenience, Care. The recognized industry framework is Consistency, Customization, Convenience, Communication, Competence, Commitment, Community, Credibility. Replace the list (or switch to the 4 C's — see MISSING #1).
2. **Bain/HBR attribution is muddled** — Introduction — "The much-cited Bain research agrees: …per the Harvard Business Review" frames Bain and HBR as separate sources that happen to agree. The HBR article IS the Bain/Reichheld research. Rewrite as "Bain partner Frederick Reichheld, writing in HBR, found…"
3. **27% / 62% repeat-purchase stat has no source** — Introduction — the figure is real (Smile.io is the origin) but the article presents it as an unattributed claim. Add the citation.
4. **Truncated bullet in measurement section** — "High-margin or B2B business: It tells you before going too far what you can afford to spend on retention, and after that," — sentence ends with a comma. The only segment in the list without a recommendation. Finish the thought (typically: CLV + NPS).
5. **"These two complement the 3 R's" references five metrics** — measurement section — previous sentence lists five metrics; "These two" has no antecedent.
6. **"Surprise" example is actually a scheduled promotion** — Strategy 8 — the salon's "complimentary deep conditioning after the 5th visit" contradicts the section's own warning ("Once all the surprises are announced, it's a promotion"). A 5th-visit reward is a punch card.
7. **Confused "loyalty manifests" sentence** — measurement section — "Loyalty manifests itself in repeat customers, increased spend, referrals, and complaints, rather than no purchases at all, and a preference…" — multiple ideas jammed together with broken connective grammar.
8. **Post-purchase section opens with awkward, off-frame sentence** — Strategy 3 — "The first time visitors visit an online store, they can be hooked by the small post-purchases they're given." Post-purchase happens AFTER buying, not when "visiting," and "first time visitors visit" repeats "visit" awkwardly.
9. **Community paragraph is passive and confused** — Strategy 5 — "They aim to" with no antecedent; "could be shared with the meal prep business" reverses direction (the business does the sharing); every example sentence uses passive voice.
10. **Duplicate "thin margins" paragraphs** — Budget section — two consecutive paragraphs say the same thing; one has broken grammar.
11. **Image1 lifecycle infographic labels don't match body text** — Advocate column starts with "Feedback" (which is strategy #7, not Advocate); First Sale uses "Personal follow-up" vs body "personalization."

**MISSING** (brief targets absent — fill second):
1. **4 C's of customer loyalty FAQ** — brief's "Questions to Answer" list + supporting keyword (110/mo). Currently replaced (badly) with 8 C's.
2. **3 R's of customer loyalty FAQ** — brief's "Questions to Answer" list. Body mentions the 3 R's briefly but no FAQ captures the PAA.
3. **Loyalty mistakes / pitfalls section** — flagged as a content GAP and unique angle in the brief's competitor heading map. Article has no such section.
4. **Five must-include keywords never appear**: "building customer loyalty" (250/mo), "customer loyalty strategies" (600/mo), "how to increase customer loyalty" (200/mo), "customer retention strategies" (1,800/mo), "how to build brand loyalty" (350/mo).

**WEAK** (content present but compromised — deepen third):
1. **Conclusion Week 3 line is broken** — "to make it seem like it is a choice" doesn't parse — what choice, to whom?
2. **Duplicate sentence in "How long does it take" FAQ** — same sentence written twice, second copy with worse grammar.
