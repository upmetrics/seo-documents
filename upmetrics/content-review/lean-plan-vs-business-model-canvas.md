1. **Thesis reversal** — H2 "Which one can be the base for your detailed plan? (spoiler: lean plan)" — Re-argue from the BMC-as-base angle per the brief; flip the heading.
2. **Body vs FAQ contradiction** — FAQ Q2 ("Which canvas do investors and accelerators prefer to see?") + Q1 — Once the body flips, FAQ becomes the punchline; until then, body and FAQ contradict each other.
3. **Internal logical contradiction** — "The real difference: 4 blocks that decide everything" → "Which one can be the base..." — Article says lean drops operations/team/marketing because the user has no operations to describe, then says lean is the better base for a plan that requires those exact chapters. Pick a side.
4. **Broken sentence** — "The real difference: 4 blocks that decide everything" — "The four-block lean canvas removes the plan almost exactly to the Operations, Supply Chain, Team, and Marketing chapters" — doesn't parse; rewrite.

6. **Anti-thesis sentence inside the BMC section** — "Where BMC just doesn't work?" — "Comparatively, a lean canvas is more likely to be (easily) translated into a traditional business plan" — directly contradicts the brief; flip with the rest of the thesis.
