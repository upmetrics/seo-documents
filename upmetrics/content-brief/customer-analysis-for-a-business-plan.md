### UGC Insights — Reddit & Quora

**Source 1:** [r/consulting — Understanding how to analyze this set of customer data?](https://www.reddit.com/r/consulting/comments/1c3cp7l/understanding_how_to_analyze_this_set_of_customer/) (Position #10 in SERP)
**Engagement:** 1 upvote, 6 comments (top reply has 27 upvotes)

**Top User Pain Points (in their words):**
1. *"I've been given the task… to understand what the root cause might be for low customer satisfaction… I ran correlation test and it was low for all variables… I didn't know how to interpret this further"* — founders/analysts overwhelmed by messy customer data with no framework.
2. *"He proposed I segment the customer satisfaction between very unhappy, unhappy, neutral and happy"* — pain around how to segment when you don't have clean buckets.
3. Top reply (27 upvotes): *"have you tried actually looking at the data? Sort it by the most unhappy customers and see if anything sticks out: any specific type of product, price of the product, age of the customers, geography, income level… You're clearly chasing a red herring."* — the dominant frustration is people running fancy stats without first eyeballing the data.

**Real Questions Users Are Asking:**
- "How do I know what's actually driving customer dissatisfaction when correlation is weak?"
- "What's the right way to segment customers when you have 100+ variables?"
- "When should I trust regression vs just sort and filter?"

**Frustrations with Existing Content:**
- Existing guides jump straight to frameworks (8-step methodology, persona templates) without showing the reader how to *interrogate the data they actually have*.
- No competitor connects "what customer analysis says" to "what you write in the business plan."

**Language Patterns to Use:**
- Users say "figure out why some customers are unhappy" not "conduct VOC analysis"
- Users say "look at the data" not "perform statistical analysis"
- Users say "segment by income level / geography / age" not "psychographic segmentation"
