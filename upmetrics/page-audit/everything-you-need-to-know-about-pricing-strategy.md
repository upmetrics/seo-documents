## 4. SERP & INTENT ANALYSIS

**What Google is rewarding (for "pricing strategy", US):**
- **Dominant intent:** Mixed informational + light commercial — readers want (a) a definition, (b) the named types of pricing strategies, (c) real examples, and (d) help choosing.
- **Content types ranking:** 8 of the 10 organic results are comprehensive blog guides (Salesforce, BDC, Paddle, DealHub, Forbes, Zapier) or encyclopedic (Wikipedia). 1 is a corporate service page (BCG). PAA box at position 3. **Position 1 is an AI Overview with 9 sitelinks** — the AI Overview is now the de-facto top of the SERP.
- **What this means:** Comprehensive, taxonomic guides win. Worked examples and named types are table stakes. Being cited as an AI Overview source is the new top-of-funnel goal — sitelinks currently go to BDC, Salesforce, Forbes, Stripe, NIQ, Zuora, Cincom, Nationwide, and business.gov.au.

**Intent match/mismatch:**
- **Current page type:** Blog guide framed for business-plan writers ("How to Write Pricing Strategy for Your Business Plan").
- **Match status:** ⚠️ **Partial mismatch for "pricing strategy" (head term); strong match for "pricing strategy business plan" (sub-query).**
- **Why:** The SERP for the head term rewards general business / SaaS / marketing guides, not business-plan-specific framing. The page's natural fit is "pricing strategy business plan" — a 5,488 imp/mo sub-query the page already ranks at pos 9.6 for. Recommendation: lean INTO the business-plan angle in title + intro, but expand the body to compete on the broader cluster (types taxonomy, brand examples, decision matrix) so it can also rank for `pricing strategy examples` (959 imp, pos 40.7) and `pricing models` (735 imp, pos 61.3).
