**Page:** [https://upmetrics.co/blog/how-to-apply-for-an-sba-disaster-loan](https://upmetrics.co/blog/how-to-apply-for-an-sba-disaster-loan)
**Primary keyword:** How to Apply for an SBA Disaster Loan
**Brand:** Upmetrics · **Geography:** United States · **Audit date:** June 23, 2026
**GSC window:** 2025-12-23 → 2026-06-20 (6 months)

> **Strategic headline:** The exact primary keyword has **zero measurable search volume** (Ahrefs vol = 0; zero GSC impressions for the phrase), and its SERP is **100% owned by SBA.gov (DR 91) + YouTube** — page-1 for the head term is neither realistic nor valuable. The page's *real* demand is a **long-tail application + problem cluster** ("sba disaster loan application," "denied," "declined," "bad credit," "credit score requirements") where it already earns impressions and is **trending up**. Two of those clusters (denial, bad credit) are **cannibalized by dedicated Upmetrics pages**. The win is to re-focus this page on the *application process* it's named for, defer denial/bad-credit intent to the dedicated pages, and deepen the thin (1,368-word) content.

### Top 3 Problems

1. **Intent & keyword mismatch:** the page is built for "how to apply" (a zero-volume head term on an unwinnable SBA.gov-owned SERP), yet its actual impressions come from a rejection/eligibility long-tail it shares with two dedicated Upmetrics pages — so it ranks for nothing well (positions mostly 40–90).

3. **Thin content for an application guide:** at 1,368 words it omits loan types, amounts/rates/terms, a real step-by-step online walkthrough, and post-submission processing/timeline detail that competitor application guides (1,730–4,701 words) all cover.

### Traffic Opportunity

> The head term has no volume, so the realistic prize is the application long-tail ("sba disaster loan application" ~67 impr/mo and growing, plus "documents needed," "application process," "how long does it take"). Positions are already **improving** (e.g., "denied" 95→22 over 6 months). Re-focusing the page on the application process and deepening it could move the application cluster from positions 40–90 into the 10–30 range and convert the current ~0 clicks into a steady trickle — while letting the dedicated denial/bad-credit pages reclaim their own queries.
