## P0 BLOCKERS

**WRONG** (factual/arithmetic errors — fix first):
1. Service-business COGS claim — "What goes into a P&L forecast?" — Services DO have COGS (contractor payouts, processing fees, per-project tools). Split guidance by self-delivery vs. subcontracting.
2. "This percent doesn't change much" — COGS% section — COGS % shifts with volume discounts, product mix, and scale. Reframe as a starting point that must be revisited quarterly.
3. Image1 (P&L vs P&L Statement table) — Image makes two claims (time period + audience for P&L Statement) that the prose never supports. Trim image or expand prose.
4. Image2 (hero title image) — Currently placed AFTER FAQ Q4. Move to top of article, below H1.
5. Image3 (Expense Checklist) — Mixes fixed and variable costs in one unlabeled list; 2 items ("Licenses and permits", "Website hosting and domain") aren't discussed in the article.

**MISSING** (critical absent content — fill second):
1. Must-include "profit and loss forecast template" (40/mo) — Add to Conclusion CTA next to the financial model generator link.
2. Must-include "profit and loss forecast business plan" (30/mo) — Add to intro + new dedicated H2 (see below).
3. GAP: "P&L vs Cash Flow Forecast" H2 — Table-stakes topic every competitor covers; add before Conclusion.
4. GAP: "P&L forecast in a business plan" H2 — UNIQUE EDGE per brief; mentioned in passing twice but never has a dedicated section.
5. GAP: "Why build a P&L forecast?" section — Table-stakes per brief; article skips the "why bother" question a first-time founder will ask.

**WEAK** (too shallow — deepen third):
1. Must-include "forecasted profit and loss statement" (60/mo) — Appears once, framed as jargon to fear. Add a defining sentence in the "What is…" H2.

**STRUCTURE:**
1. "How to create a profit and loss forecast in 5 steps?" section is 1,085 words (4.2× section avg) under a single H2 with five H3 sub-steps. Promote each step from H3 to H2 so the outline is scannable.
