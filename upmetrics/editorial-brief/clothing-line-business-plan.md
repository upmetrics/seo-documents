## 2. Thesis & Key Messages

### Thesis Statement

> Verra & Linden Apparel Co., LLC seeks a $185,000 SBA 7(a) loan to launch a direct-to-consumer sustainable women's apparel brand in Tipton Heights, Oregon, projecting break-even by month 18 and $679,630 in revenue with positive net income by Year 3.

### Key Messages

1. The founders bring complementary, directly relevant experience: nine years in apparel product development (Caldwell) and six years in DTC growth marketing (Reyes), eliminating the two most common failure modes for new clothing brands — bad product and bad marketing.
2. The market opportunity is concrete and supported by current data: U.S. women's apparel is a $195+ billion market, and 62% of consumers report willingness to pay more for sustainably produced apparel — a segment growing at 8–10% annually versus 2–3% for the broader apparel category.
3. The financial model is conservative and loan-ready: 60%+ gross margins, break-even at 4,832 orders/year, Year 2 DSCR of 1.28x and Year 3 DSCR of 1.45x — both above the SBA 7(a) 1.15x threshold.
4. The operational model deliberately avoids the two highest-risk choices for new clothing brands: no physical retail in Year 1–2, and no inventory financing through wholesale — every dollar of inventory is paid down by direct-to-consumer sales.
5. The use of funds is specific and defensible: $55,000 in capitalized equipment and studio buildout, $40,000 in launch inventory, $30,000 in launch marketing and setup, and $125,000 in working capital reserve — enough cash to cover ~12 months of fixed operating expenses if revenue underperforms.
