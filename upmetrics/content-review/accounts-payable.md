**WRONG** (factual/arithmetic errors — fix first):
1. $50K / 10 days / 10% cost-of-capital math → "How AP affects cash flow" → $50,000 × 10/365 = $1,370 of dollar-days in the business; the 10% cost-of-capital framing lands you at ~$137 in annualized financing savings, not $1,370. Pick one framing and make the math match (Comment 1).
2. Two separate $1,200 examples (AWS bill + utility bill) within consecutive H2s → "How AP works" + "Entry 1" → Change one of them; reads like a copy-paste oversight (Comment 2).
3. IRS Publication 535 cited for accrual-vs-cash → "Accrual basis" paragraph → Pub 535 was retired after 2022. Swap to Pub 538 and update the hyperlink target (Comment 3).
4. Image2 (Cash Flow screenshot) highlights "Cash Out" but the text is about AP **adding back** to operating cash → re-shoot or change highlight (Comment 18).
5. Image4 (AP report screenshot) shows every AP row at $0 because Purchases on Credit = 0% in the source forecast → re-capture from a forecast where Purchases on Credit > 0% (Comment 19).
6. Image5 (Balance Sheet screenshot) shows $0 AP across all months while the text claims "your AP balance grows" → re-capture from same non-zero forecast (Comment 20).
7. Image6 (hero infographic) is sitting at the bottom of the FAQs instead of the top of the article → move to top / set as featured image (Comment 21).

**MISSING** (critical absent content — fill second):

2. Categorized real-world AP examples block → Under "What is accounts payable?" or its own H3 → Add a 6-8 item bulleted list (rent, utilities, SaaS, contractors, inventory on credit, professional services, freight, marketing). Featured-snippet bait (Comment 10).

4. Must-include keyword "accounts payable definition" (4,800 vol) → "What is accounts payable?" opening sentence → Currently 0 occurrences in body; weave it in naturally or add a TL;DR callout (Comment 13).
5. Internal link to live sister article /blog/accounts-receivable → AP-vs-AR section, paragraph 2 → Anchor "Accounts receivable" (Comment 16).

**WEAK** (too shallow — deepen third):
1. AFP 2024 fraud stat is hedged as "the majority of organizations" → Step 2 → Brief specifically asked for the actual figure (80%); pull and cite (Comment 4).
2. DPO formula uses COGS without caveat for service businesses → "Key accounts payable metrics" → Add one-line caveat: substitute total operating purchases for service businesses, otherwise DPO is misleadingly high (Comment 7).
