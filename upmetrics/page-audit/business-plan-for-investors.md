## 4. SERP & INTENT ANALYSIS

### 4A. Search Intent Classification

**Intent:** Informational, with strong commercial undercurrent. Searchers are usually founders preparing to fundraise — they want **a step-by-step framework, examples, and a deliverable they can copy** (template, outline, sample). This is high-stakes content: getting it wrong means failing to raise.

**Confirmed by:**
- 6 of top 10 SERP results are "how-to" guides (startups.com, advantagecap, yousign, prometai, HBR, Investopedia)
- Position 6 is a downloadable PDF template (Purdue) — confirming template intent
- 130+ GSC long-tail queries are dominated by procedural phrasings ("how to write…", "how to make…", "how to create…", "preparing a…")
- AI Overview at position 1 synthesizes step-by-step content (essential sections, length, what investors want)

**Target page intent match: STRONG.** It's a step-by-step "how to write" guide that names the 12 sections. The structure aligns with intent. The problem is depth + authority, not intent mismatch.

### 4C. Featured Snippet / AI Overview Opportunity

Featured snippet is unavailable (AI Overview holds the slot). **However, AI Overview citations are still earnable** — the page must contain content that directly answers the synthesis themes:

1. A clear definitional intro: "A business plan for investors is a [specific] document that [does X]. It's typically 15-25 pages and includes [N] core sections."
2. A scannable list of essential sections (the page already has this — H3s 1-12)
3. Length guidance ("15-30 pages" — yousign + startups.com both call this out)
4. A "what investors look for" framing tied to the 4 W's: why this, why now, why you, why them (startups.com framework — the target lacks this explicit structure)
5. Cited sources — AI Overview prefers content with named citations to authoritative sources

The target has #2 already. Adding #1, #3, #4, #5 is in scope of the Update Brief.

### 4D. Intent Mismatch?

**No mismatch on the primary keyword.** The target serves the right intent. The mismatch is on **proposal-vs-plan**: 7 of the top GSC queries use "proposal" instead of "plan" (collectively ~1,400 impressions/mo). Users searching "business proposal for investors" are looking for a similar but slightly different artifact — usually shorter, with more emphasis on the deal terms (amount, equity stake, use of funds) than the operating plan.

**Recommendation:** Add a short "Plan vs Proposal: what's the difference?" section that names both, explains when each is used, and clarifies that the same 12-section structure applies to both with minor framing changes. Handled in Update Brief §14E as a NEW section.
