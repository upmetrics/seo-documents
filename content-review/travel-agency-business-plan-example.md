## What's Working Well

- **Dual revenue model is compelling.** The leisure-corporate split with clear operational ownership (Nicole = leisure, Marcus = corporate) is one of the strongest structural elements in the plan. It avoids the common trap of two founders doing everything.
- **Financial model is internally consistent.** Despite the admin cost omission, every other calculation checks out: break-even, DSCR, balance sheets, cash flow chains, depreciation schedules. This level of rigor is rare in sample plans.
- **Market data is well-sourced.** Every major statistic has a hyperlinked source. The Atlanta-specific data (income by ZIP, Fortune 500 count, metro population) adds local credibility that generic plans lack.
- **Honest about startup disadvantage.** The Competitive Analysis section acknowledges "no client base, no track record, and no brand recognition at launch." This candor strengthens rather than weakens the plan.
- **Writing quality is strong.** The voice is professional but approachable. Complex financial concepts are explained clearly. The reading experience flows naturally through most sections.
