### Top 3 Problems

1. **The "2026" label is cosmetic, not substantive.** The title, H1, and headings all say "2026," but the stats themselves are from 2022, 2023, and 2024 studies (HBR 2017, OnePageBusinessPlan, Indeed-cited Bplans). Competitors ranking above (electroiq at pos 2, planaipro at pos 3-ish intent) cite 2024-2025 studies and explicitly call out 2025-2026 trends.

2. **No AI angle — the single biggest 2026 conversation in business planning is missing.** Competitor 9cv9 has 4 dedicated AI FAQs; AI-powered planning tools grew +180% per electroiq; the AI Overview at SERP position 1 already pulls AI-era statistics. The target page has zero AI content. This is the gap most likely to win AI Overview citations.

3. **External citations are anchored as "Source" 64 times — and 3+ point to direct competitors.** The literal word "Source" passes no topical signal. Worse, current outbound citations include [https://blog.9cv9.com/top-100-business-plan-statistics-data-trends/](https://blog.9cv9.com/top-100-business-plan-statistics-data-trends/), [https://llcbuddy.com/data/business-plan-statistics/](https://llcbuddy.com/data/business-plan-statistics/), and [https://www.businessdasher.com/business-plan-statistics/](https://www.businessdasher.com/business-plan-statistics/) — all are competing statistics roundups, not primary sources.
