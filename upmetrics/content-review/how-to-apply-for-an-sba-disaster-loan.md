## P0 BLOCKERS

**WRONG** (factual/arithmetic errors — fix first):
1. **Interest rate is incomplete** ("can be as low as 4%") — How much can you borrow — Add the dual-rate structure (4% no-credit-elsewhere / 8% credit-elsewhere) plus the 2.875% home/renter and 3.625% nonprofit rates.
2. **Audience mix-up in county sentence** ("regardless of the number of customers you lost") — Do you qualify — Article includes homeowners/renters who don't have customers. Rewrite audience-neutral.
3. **Draft instruction box left in article** ("This is your working document...") — Top of article — Delete before publishing.
4. **Raw Google Doc URL in intro prose** ("delay or worse,https://docs.google.com/...") — Do you qualify (P1) — Remove the URL or convert "rejection" into a proper internal link.
5. **Hero infographic placed at the very end of the article** (image2.png) — After FAQ — Move to the top so it functions as the orienting overview.

**MISSING** (critical absent content — fill second):
1. **Filing deadlines (60 days / 9 months)** — Do you qualify section — The single most time-sensitive fact in this topic isn't stated anywhere.
2. **Dedicated "Types of SBA disaster loans" subsection** — Before the limits table — Brief's gap table flagged as "must add"; all 3 competitors cover it.
3. **12-month interest-free deferral period** — How much can you borrow — The most reassuring repayment fact for a stressed applicant; entirely absent.
4. **FAQ questions** — "Can I still get an SBA disaster loan? (deadlines)" + "How long does the SBA disaster loan application take?" — Both on the brief's "Questions to Answer" list, both missing from the FAQ.

**WEAK** (too shallow — deepen third):
1. **Post-submission timeline lacks time anchors** — What happens after you submit — Stages are well-organized but have no 2–12 week ranges or 5-day disbursement window. Brief named this exact section as the article's UNIQUE EDGE.
