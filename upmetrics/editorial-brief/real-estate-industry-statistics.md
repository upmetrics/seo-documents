## 2. Thesis & Key Messages

### Thesis Statement

> The 2026 US real estate market is no longer the post-pandemic seller's market of 2021-2023 — inventory is recovering, price growth has cooled to low single digits, agent commission structures have been rewritten by the NAR settlement, and tech adoption (video, drone, AI) is now the dividing line between agents who close and agents who don't.

### Key Messages

1. The market is **rebalancing, not crashing** — sales volume and inventory are both rising while price growth has fallen to ~2% YoY (down from double digits in 2021-22).
2. The **post-NAR-settlement commission landscape** is now a measurable shift, not a rumor — buyer agreements, fee transparency, and competing compensation models are reshaping how agents earn.
3. **Buyer demographics have decisively skewed Millennial/Gen-Z** — 38% of buyers in 2025 were Millennials, the largest share on record, with structurally different demand patterns (smaller homes, urban-suburban hybrids, remote-work-flexible locations).
4. **Marketing tech adoption now correlates with deal velocity** — video listings get 403% more inquiries (NAR), drone photography helps homes sell 68% faster (Matterport) — yet only ~26% of agents use video consistently.
5. **Commercial real estate is diverging sharply** — office vacancy remains elevated (~20% in major metros) while industrial and multifamily are still tight. The "CRE" headline hides three different stories.

## 8. Content Outline

> This outline is informed by reference article analysis and editorial judgment. Every section is justified by audience need, thesis support, or content-type best practices.

### Writing Tone

**For Statistics Roundup:**
- Write like a data journalist — let the numbers tell the story.
- Every stat needs an inline source citation (e.g., "(Source: NAR, May 2026)") and a "so what" interpretation sentence.
- Group stats into themes, not random lists. Each section is a mini-narrative, not a bullet dump.
- Provide context: "X% — up from Y% last year" or "X% — the highest since [date]". A naked number without a comparison adds nothing.
- Use the word "percent" or "%" consistently — pick one. Same for dollar formatting.
- Year reference: this article is the **2026 update**. Default time frame is the most recent month/quarter available at publish (likely May or June 2026 data).

**General rules:**
- **Target reading level:** Grade 8-9 (Flesch-Kincaid).
- Plain language, contractions, "you/your".
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max).
- Real examples, numbers, specifics.
- NO filler sentences, NO passive voice, NO AI buzzwords (see Banned AI Terms in the Appendix).
- **Stat verification:** Every cited stat must be verified against its primary source at the time of writing. The data in this brief is current as of June 2026 — re-verify any figure that's older than 60 days when you actually write the piece.

- 🔴 **MUST HAVE** — Non-negotiable. Essential to the thesis or content type.
- 🟡 **SHOULD HAVE** — Important for depth and reader value.
- 🟢 **NICE TO HAVE** — Adds differentiation but not essential.
