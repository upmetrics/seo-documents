## 2. Thesis & Key Messages

### Thesis Statement

> Summit Strength Fitness LLC seeks a $450,000 SBA 7(a) loan to open a 10,000-square-foot full-service gym in Northgate, Ohio, reaching 600 members and $480,000 revenue in Year 1 and turning profitable by Year 3 — this plan shows founders exactly how a lender-ready gym plan is structured and financed.

### Key Messages

1. A credible gym plan leads with numbers a lender can underwrite — startup costs, source of funds, a 3-year P&L, and a break-even that ties to member count.
2. Gyms are capital-intensive: equipment and buildout drive most of the $650K startup budget, and depreciation plus debt service mean profitability often arrives in Year 3, not Year 1.
3. Recurring membership revenue is the engine — retention and blended revenue per member matter more than headline sign-up numbers.
4. Every chapter should read like it was written for a gym specifically: member tiers, class scheduling, trainer staffing, and equipment layout — not a generic template with "gym" pasted in.
5. Conservative, internally consistent financials build lender trust; hockey-stick projections destroy it.

## 8. Content Outline

### Writing Tone

**For Sample Business Plan:**
- Write like a professional business consultant drafting a formal plan for a client.
- Third-person throughout — "Summit Strength Fitness will..." not "We will..." or "I will..."
- Every claim backed by a specific number, date, or named detail.
- Financial sections use the actual pre-calculated tables from Section 6B — do NOT invent new numbers.
- Replace vague language with specifics: "15% year-over-year revenue growth," not "significant growth."
- Use gym-industry vocabulary: active members, churn, blended dues, member acquisition cost, class utilization, floor supervision.
- Maintain consistent use of the business name, owner names (Marcus Bennett, Rachel Torres), and location (Northgate, Ohio) throughout.

**General rules:**
- Target reading level: Grade 8-9. Plain language, contractions, "you/your" only in reader-facing CTA asides.
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences). Real numbers and specifics. No filler, no passive voice, no AI buzzwords (see Appendix banned list).

### Section Priority Labels

- 🔴 **MUST HAVE** — Essential to a lender-ready plan.
- 🟡 **SHOULD HAVE** — Adds depth and credibility.
- 🟢 **NICE TO HAVE** — Extra differentiation.
