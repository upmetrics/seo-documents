## 2. Thesis & Key Messages

### Thesis Statement

> Frostline Creamery seeks a $250,000 SBA 7(a) loan to launch a craft retail ice cream shop in Brookfield, Wisconsin, anchored by an in-house production kitchen, a custom-cake program, and a private-event catering line — with a path to $640,976 in Year 3 revenue and break-even profitability by Year 2.

### Key Messages

1. The Brookfield trade area is underserved for craft frozen dessert: zero independent in-house batch producers within a 7-mile radius, with consumer demand consistently above national per-capita averages (Wisconsin ranks #2 nationally for ice cream consumption per capita per IDFA data).
2. Frostline's three-revenue-stream model (retail scoops + custom cakes + private events) reduces seasonal exposure compared to a scoops-only shop and supports stronger Q4–Q1 cash flow.
3. The ownership team combines proven multi-unit retail operations experience (Maya — 8 years) with formal pastry training and Penn State Ice Cream Short Course credentials (Daniel) — a pairing rarely found in independent ice cream shops.
4. The $250K SBA 7(a) loan structure (10-year term, 8.5% fixed) covers all capital expenditures and a $80K working-capital float, leaving owners with $80K equity in the business at launch and a debt service ratio that is supportable from Year 2 forward.
5. The financial model is conservative — Year 1 projects a $40,946 net loss (a typical first-year reality for brick-and-mortar food operations after depreciation and one-time launch expenses), with positive net income from Year 2 onward and ending cash growing every year.

**Borrow:**

- Reference 3's industry stat-citation pattern — pull stats from IDFA, NICRA, NRA, and similar bodies to ground the Market Analysis chapter.

**Avoid:**
- Reference 1's dated tone ("the Shoppe", "Augustus Herman Dwyer") and marketing thinness — our plan is a 2026 launch and must reflect current digital/social marketing realities.
