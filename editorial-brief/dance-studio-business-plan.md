## 2. Thesis & Key Messages

### Thesis Statement

> Rhythm & Grace Dance Academy is a financially sound, community-rooted dance studio positioned to serve an underserved suburban Georgia market — and this business plan demonstrates, with real numbers and industry-specific detail, exactly how it gets from Day 1 to profitability.

### Key Messages

1. Dance studio financials are not generic service-business financials — monthly tuition cohorts, recital revenue spikes, and instructor cost structure require a model built specifically for this industry.
2. The Maplecrest market has meaningful unmet demand: the nearest competing multi-style studio is 14 miles away, and the local population of school-age children (est. 12,400) exceeds what one competitor studio can absorb.
3. Year 1 operates at a net loss — this is expected, planned for, and accounted for in the SBA loan sizing. The studio reaches EBITDA breakeven at 132 enrolled students, which Year 2 exceeds comfortably.
4. The annual recital is not just a performance event — it's the studio's single most powerful retention and referral driver, with 80%+ of students re-enrolling for the following semester after participating.
5. Instructor cost is the largest variable expense (38% of revenue) and the lever that determines long-term margin — the staffing model at Rhythm & Grace is designed to keep this ratio stable as enrollment grows, without sacrificing class quality.

## 8. Content Outline

### Writing Tone

**For Sample Business Plan:**
- Write in the third person throughout. "Rhythm & Grace Dance Academy will..." — not "we will" or "I will"
- Every claim requires a specific number, date, or named individual. "Enrollment is projected to reach 125 students by Month 12" — not "enrollment is expected to grow significantly"
- Financial sections must include the actual pre-calculated tables from Section 6B above — do NOT invent new numbers or modify any figures
- Use dance industry vocabulary: enrollment-months, per-student tuition, recital registration, summer intensive, class cohort, direct instructor cost. A bank lending to a dance studio should feel like this plan was written by someone who understands the industry
- No filler sentences. No passive voice. No phrases from the Banned AI Terms list in the Appendix

**General rules:**
- Target reading level: Grade 8–9 (Flesch-Kincaid)
- Short paragraphs: 3–4 sentences max
- Bold key figures on first appearance in each chapter (e.g., **$198,000**, **90 students**, **$130,000 SBA loan**)
- No fluff in the intro — start with the business, not with motivational framing

- 🔴 **MUST HAVE** — Non-negotiable. Essential to the thesis or content type. Cutting this = incomplete article.
- 🟡 **SHOULD HAVE** — Important for depth and reader value. Cutting this weakens the article but doesn't break it.
- 🟢 **NICE TO HAVE** — Adds differentiation or extra value but not essential.
