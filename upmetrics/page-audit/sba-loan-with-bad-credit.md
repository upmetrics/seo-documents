1. **Zero page-level link equity.** The page has **0 backlinks and 0 referring domains** while every page-1 organic result sits on a DR 57–91 domain (sba.gov DR 91, bankrate DR 90, lendio DR 75). With no link authority, the page physically cannot climb past page 3 — it's stuck at positions 28–85 for every query in the cluster.
2. **Significant content gaps vs. page-1 competitors.** The two strongest external rankers (1West, OnDeck) and Upmetrics' own [what-is-an-sba-loan](https://upmetrics.co/blog/what-is-an-sba-loan) page all carry an **eligibility-requirements checklist, a FICO/credit-score deep dive, real lender/rate tables, and a "common mistakes to avoid" section** — none of which exist on the target page.
3. **Keyword cannibalization across 3 Upmetrics pages.** [/blog/how-to-get-a-business-loan-with-bad-credit](https://upmetrics.co/blog/how-to-get-a-business-loan-with-bad-credit) and [/blog/what-is-an-sba-loan](https://upmetrics.co/blog/what-is-an-sba-loan) both rank for the same SBA-bad-credit queries — the business-loan page even **outranks the target on "bad credit sba loans" (pos 14 vs 36)** — splitting authority and confusing Google about which page to surface.

### Traffic Opportunity

> The exact primary keyword is low-volume (40/mo), but the **cluster** is real: **376 queries, 2,635 impressions, and 0 clicks** over 6 months — the page generates demand but converts none of it because it never reaches page 1. Fixing link equity + content depth + cannibalization could move the cluster from ~position 35 into the top 10, realistically worth **~40–120 clicks/month**, with more if it breaks into the top 3.

## 4. SERP & INTENT ANALYSIS

**What Google is rewarding:**
- **Dominant intent:** Informational + commercial-investigation. Searchers want to know *whether* it's possible, *how*, and *which* programs accept low credit — before they consider a lender.
- **Content types ranking:** Top 3 = official authority ([sba.gov](https://www.sba.gov/funding-programs/loans) loans + microloans, DR 91) with a PAA block at position 2; positions 4–10 = a mix of educational blog guides ([lendio](https://www.lendio.com/blog/sba-loan-options-with-bad-credit) DR 75, [bankrate](https://www.bankrate.com/loans/small-business/where-to-get-bad-credit-business-loan/) DR 90, [1West](https://www.1west.com/how-to-get-an-sba-loan-with-bad-credit-a-realistic-guide-for-small-business-owners/) DR 57, [ondeck](https://www.ondeck.com/resources/sba-loan-with-bad-credit) DR 73), one product/landing page ([Bank of America](https://www.bankofamerica.com/smallbusiness/business-financing/sba-financing/)), one solutions page ([National Funding](https://www.nationalfunding.com/solutions/bad-credit-small-business-loans/)), and **3 UGC threads (2 Reddit + 1 Quora) at position 6**.
- **What this means:** Google rewards comprehensive, data-backed educational guides — but the heavy PAA presence and 3 UGC threads signal strong demand for **direct, plain-language answers to specific questions** (credit-score thresholds, what disqualifies you). A guide that answers those crisply has an edge.

**Intent match/mismatch:**
- **Current page type:** Blog article / how-to guide.
- **Match status:** ✅ Match — the target is the same content type as the page-1 guides.
- **Why it still loses:** The mismatch isn't *intent*, it's **authority + depth**. The minimum DR among ranking guides is ~57; the target has 0 backlinks. The page-1 guides also carry data the target lacks (FICO breakdowns, eligibility checklists, approval stats) — so even on parity of link equity, the content is thinner.
