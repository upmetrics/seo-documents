1. **Ranking failure on high-impression queries** — the page surfaces for "business plan executive summary example" (665 impressions), "…example startup" (690), "…sample" (605), and "business plan executive summary" (375), but sits at average positions 47–51 with a **0% CTR**. At the page's current traffic potential of 1,500/month (Ahrefs) and KD 29, the ceiling justifies a full rewrite rather than incremental edits.

## 4. SERP & Intent Analysis

### 4A. Intent Classification

**Informational with strong "example-seeking" transactional undertone.** URL analysis of the top 10:
- **How-to guides:** SBA (#4), Forbes (#2), NerdWallet (AI-Overview cited), LivePlan, Xero, US Chamber, Coursera (#7)
- **Example-heavy pages:** Forbes (#2 — "Example And Template"), Asana (#9 — "Examples: How to Write + Template"), the UVM PDF (#8 — actual sample)
- **University/library pages:** USF Libguides (#6), SFU (#10)

Google is rewarding **"guide + example + template"** hybrid pages. Pure how-to without an example ranks poorly (target page's main angle). The slug, URL, and 6 out of 7 highest-impression queries all contain "example" or "sample" — users who see the Upmetrics page in results are specifically searching for an example they can copy.

### 4B. Intent Mismatch — the Core Problem

The target page headlines itself as a generic how-to ("How to Write a Great Executive Summary…"). The example itself is a single H2 section — it is **not** the primary framing. Users searching for an example scroll to it or bounce.

**Fix framing:** Lead with a visual, scannable example at the top (above the fold); then the 9-step framework; then the template download. This mirrors the Forbes #2 result and matches the transactional intent embedded in the keyword.

### 6C. Topic Gap Detail

Five content gaps worth adding (full writer guidance lives in the Content Update Brief Section 14E):

1. **"The 5 parts of an executive summary"** — directly answers the top PAA question. Competitors Asana, LivePlan, and Xero all own this framing. Add as an H2 early in the article, before the 9-step how-to.

4. **"Milestones and traction"** — LivePlan's 9th section. Investor-focused; missing from Upmetrics's 9-step framework. Add as step 9 or a sub-section of financials.
5. **"Writing for different audiences: investors vs banks vs internal readers"** — Xero's differentiator. One H3 with three short paragraphs. Positions the article for investor-pitch searchers.
