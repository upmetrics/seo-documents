## P0 BLOCKERS

**WRONG** (factual/arithmetic errors — fix first):
1. Conclusion verbatim duplicate — The closing sentence of P1 is repeated as a standalone paragraph P2.
2. Hero image at end — image2.png (the article cover) sits after the final FAQ answer instead of above the introduction.
3. image1.png Stage 1/2/3 framework not in text — Image introduces specific stages (2 weeks → 1 month → 3 months of expenses, with ~3/~6/~12-18 month buildouts at 5% of $20K/month) that the surrounding prose never describes.
4. "Three things that reduce the impact:" — Only two bullets follow in Unplanned Large Purchases.

**MISSING** (critical absent content — fill second):
1. Definition of "cash flow problem" — Gap table marks this as a foundational differentiator; article has no definition paragraph.
2. 7 of 9 must-include keywords absent from body — business cash flow problems, how to solve cash flow problems, cash flow problems in small business, cash flow problems and solutions, dealing with cash flow problems, cash flow issues, causes of cash flow problems.
3. Two brief-required FAQ questions missing — "What are cash flow problems and why do they happen?" and "How to solve cash flow problems in a small business?"
4. Internal link to "How to Forecast Cash Flow" — Listed as required in the brief; not used anywhere.
5. SBA.gov link — FAQ cites SBA's 3-month recommendation but there's no link, despite SBA.gov being a required external source in the brief.
6. U.S. Bank 82% "study" link points to usbank.com homepage — No actual study page supports the figure.

**WEAK** (too shallow — deepen third):
1. Mixing Personal and Business Finances — 78 words despite being a "Gap — unique" differentiator. No concrete separation steps, no tax/audit risk, no "reasonable owner salary" guidance.
2. Low Profit Margins section conflates gross margin with cash flow — 25-50% / 50-75% / 70-85% figures are gross margins, but the cash risk comes from thin net margin + AR timing.
3. Overdependence 25% rule stated as absolute — No citation, debatable as a universal threshold.
4. Seasonal Revenue Swings Census Bureau citation is vague — "disproportionate share" with no percentage.
5. Nike 2022 inventory example is missing the number — Reuters article (which is linked) reports inventory up ~44% YoY to ~$9.7B; article drops the figure.
6. Prevention section — 5 steps of 1 sentence each in the article's unique-differentiator section.
