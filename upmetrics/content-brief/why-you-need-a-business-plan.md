## 2. Why We'll Win

> Same structural shape as LivePlan + SBA. Differentiation comes from original citations, deeper operational sections, and engagement with founder objections that competitors duck.

- **Real numbers at every claim, not vague promises** — Competitors say "plans help raise capital" without numbers. We cite specific stats (success rate %, funding ratios, dollar-anchored examples) inline at each reason. Pull from `/blog/business-plan-statistics` and SBA/Census sources.
- **A 4-row review-cadence table competitors don't have** — All references say "review your plan" without telling readers what to actually check or when. We add a Monthly / Quarterly / Annual / Triggered table inside Reason #14 (baseline measurement). This is the LivePlan-mirror's biggest operational gap.
- **Brief engagement with the "I'm bootstrapped, do I need this?" objection** — None of LivePlan, SBA, WaveApps, or CRIAdv address this directly. We answer it inside FAQ + Reason #11 (hiring) with concrete bootstrap-relevant use cases. Honest, brief — not a full counterargument that breaks the listicle structure.
- **AI-era framing without the hype** — We acknowledge AI-assisted planning is now standard (Upmetrics uses it). Each reason notes what AI accelerates vs. what still needs founder judgment. Competitors are silent on this.
- **15 reasons with no filler** — LivePlan-style mirror, but every reason gets a substantive stat or example. No "you'll sleep better" reasons. Cut what's thin.

## 11. Writing Tone — DO / DON'T

> Why this matters: this article competes against vendor blogs and government style — easy to drift into either marketing speak or bureaucratic prose. The voice that actually wins this query is **direct, founder-aware, lightly skeptical, willing to acknowledge the "do I really need this?" objection.**

**DO:**
- Address the reader directly ("you", "your business")
- Use specific dollar amounts and percentages when citing
- Acknowledge skeptical framings before resolving them ("Yes, plans go out of date — that's why review cadence matters")
- Use plain verbs (write, change, raise, hire, decide)
- Vary sentence length — short punches between longer paragraphs
- Cite at the claim, not in a "research shows" footer paragraph

**DON'T:**
- Use any banned AI terms (see appendix below)
- Write filler reasons like "you'll sleep better" or "it's easier than you think"
- Lecture the reader about why planning is virtuous
- Use vendor-speak ("leverage your strategic blueprint")
- Bundle citations into a single "research shows it works" paragraph
- Treat "review your plan" as a complete instruction — operationalize it
