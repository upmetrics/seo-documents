## 2. Thesis & Key Messages

### Thesis Statement

> SunHaven Tanning & Wellness seeks a $135,000 SBA 7(a) loan to launch a hybrid UV/sunless/red-light salon in Crestview Falls, Iowa, generating $361,962 in Year 1 revenue through a membership-led model with 58.3% gross margins and a Year 2 break-even buffer of 39%.

### Key Messages

1. The US tanning salon industry is consolidating — operators that diversify beyond UV-only services are gaining share from those that don't.
2. A membership-led revenue mix (target: 50% of revenue from recurring memberships) flattens seasonality and improves cash predictability.
3. Owner-operators with prior salon experience are a meaningful credit-quality signal for SBA lenders.
4. Hybrid service offerings (UV + sunless + red light) reduce regulatory risk by lowering dependence on UV revenue, which faces ongoing health-policy headwinds.
5. The plan turns a small profit in Year 1 ($2,371) and grows to $54,622 in Year 3 — financial trajectory matches what a community lender expects to see.

## Why This Matters

- The US tanning salon market is $1.9B in 2026 (IBISWorld), but business count fell at a 4.5% CAGR over 2020–2025 — refreshed planning is the difference between operators that survive and operators that close.
- Industry has pivoted from UV-only to hybrid (sunless, red-light, wellness) — a 2023-vintage sample plan likely under-represents this shift and reads as outdated to modern lenders.
- SBA lenders and community banks now expect plans that explicitly address UV health/safety regulation — not as a footnote, but as a risk-mitigation strategy.
- Membership-model salons consistently outperform session-only operators on retention and unit economics — a refreshed sample plan should make this revenue mix the centerpiece, not an afterthought.
- Year-round demand pattern (event/wedding spikes plus winter base) shapes financials in a way generic templates miss — the operating plan and break-even need to reflect this.
