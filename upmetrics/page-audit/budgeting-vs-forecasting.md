## 4. SERP & INTENT ANALYSIS

### What Google is rewarding

- **Dominant intent:** Informational — comparison + definition. The query is "and" not "vs", but users are asking for the differences AND how to do both.
- **Content types ranking:**
  - 7/10 organic results are blog guides / explainers
  - 1 is a Q&A explainer (Investopedia at #2)
  - 1 is a CPE webcast (AICPA at #6 — not a content competitor)
  - 1 is a topic page from a vendor (IBM at #9)
  - The AI Overview cites Investopedia, a YouTube video, and Rippling
- **What this means:** Google rewards comprehensive, mid-length comparison guides that go beyond "definition + 4 differences" — they also cover benefits, types, methods, challenges, and a process for doing both. The current target page is the lightest content on the SERP.

### Intent match/mismatch

- **Current page type:** Blog comparison article (definitional)
- **Match status:** ⚠️ **Partial mismatch** — the target page covers definitions + 4 differences + 1 example pair + a brief "how to prepare" section + 5 FAQs. It's directionally correct but **missing the "and" half of the query.** Top-ranking competitors all explain how budgeting and forecasting work *together* and provide a step-by-step process; the target page treats them as separate concepts, almost without showing how they integrate.
- **Specific gap:** The target page has zero coverage of "benefits of budgeting", "benefits of forecasting", "types of budgets", "types of forecasts", "common challenges", or "why you need both" — all of which 3+ competitors cover.
