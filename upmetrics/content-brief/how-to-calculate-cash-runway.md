## 2. Why We'll Win

- **Decision-making depth no competitor delivers.** CFI, WallStreetPrep, and Rho stop at "calculate it and extend it." JPMorgan goes deeper but treats every founder identically. Our angle — anchored to the underlying founder questions — owns interpretation, decision frameworks, and stage-by-stage benchmarks.
- **Stage × situation benchmark table is a genuine gap.** Every competitor gives one number ("12-18 months" or "24-36 months") and stops. We'll publish a table mapping benchmark runway by stage (pre-seed → Series B+) and situation (bootstrapped, post-raise, planning a raise, in a downturn) — directly answering "how much runway should I have?"
- **Calculation-error troubleshooting section is unique.** No competitor covers "what to do if your runway number looks wrong" — yet that's the first thing every founder asks. We'll include a checklist of the 6 most common calculation mistakes (line of credit misuse, deferred revenue, one-off expenses, capitalized vs operating, seasonal burn).
- **Default alive vs default dead made operational.** Rho mentions Paul Graham's concept; we'll operationalize it with a 2-minute self-test the reader can run on their own numbers.
- **Two-way internal linking with the existing burn rate article cleans up cannibalization** and signals topic ownership to Google. The new article inherits runway queries; the burn rate article keeps burn queries.
